What are Pastured eggs?

Pastured eggs are eggs that are layed by hens that have access to real, fresh pasture grass on a daily basis. Although a diet of bugs, worms, insects, as well as herbs, grass and seeds doesn’t sound appealing to us, to a hen, it’s a heavenly buffet. Making sure our hens are moved to get as much as they can eat on a daily basis makes a difference.

So, are these the same as free range?

Not even close. Chickens can do amazing things to grassy land. They fertilize it, break it up, rid it of parasites, and do all sorts of other good things. However, in doing so, they can also make it a barren wasteland fairly quickly. Free range chickens are often just hens that are free of cages, but that spend most of their time in the same area, which usually means no more grass, no more bugs, and none of the healthy things chickens love.

What are the benefits of pastured eggs?

Some reports have shown that pastured eggs can have 1/3 less cholesterol, 1/4 less saturated fat, 2/3 MORE vitamin A, 2 times the omega-3 fatty acids, 3 times more vitamin E, and up to 7 times more beta carotene. The bright color of the yolks indicates the significantly higher beta carotene levels and the taste is much better in our experience (though some would disagree with the objectivity of that).
